Verbena Veneris

Skills: Graphic Design, Brand Identity, Illustration

Software: Adobe Illustrator, Adobe Photoshop


Verbena Veneris is a small business that provides homemade tea blends, esoteric goods, coffee, and with it’s sister-brand Isis&Pedro, handcrafted goods.

The founder wanted the logo, packaging and accompanying graphics for the brand to show their main concerns and inspirations, without limiting the scope of what they can offer.

Those sources of inspiration were nature, mysticism, and a deep respect for body/mind connection.

To represent them, I opted for a selection of flowers commonly used by the brand for tea blends, arranged in a circle, to represent the changes in season, and the cyclical nature of life.

Concerns over the environment were also taken into consideration for the packaging design - it’s far more likely that people will want to keep and re-use something that’s beautiful.

To compliment the decorative nature of the logo, an intricate floral pattern was created, to adorn the book-marker style labels, reusable boxes and re-sealable paper bags.

Last touches included two custom icons for the healing and magical properties of tea and herbal blend.

Work and development of the brand continues with the creation of market banners and other assets as the brand develops.

Solamen Scriptorium

Skills: Brand Design, Web Design, HTML, CSS

Software: WordPress, Figma, CPanel


Solamen Scriptorium is a personal project that focused on creating an online location to share art projects, development of artistic side-projects, and tutorials.

I wanted the branding and layout to combine my passions for nature and medieval art, without falling into overly stereotypical depictions, focusing instead on creating a modern, easy to navigate design, with a warm and welcoming aesthetic.

As an artist’s website, it could not exist without a gallery that would quickly and clearly display artworks, link to support channels, online store and a newsletter sign-up form.

Another important feature of this website, was the creation of a blog, where project updates, tutorials could be shared and leads could be driven to the website.

Navigability was also a big concern, as I find it’s a must-have to make content and information as easy as possible to find on any website.
